1986

Hewlett-Packard dominated the industry for more than a decade. Their "Packard Bell" and "HP 4000" machines soon dominated large computing systems, and came to dominate the world of personal computing for more than 20 years. Their HP 2100 machine, manufactured in 1979, incorporated a groundbreaking "SCSI", "CFMA" and "CLONE" technologies. This machine and the other HP 2100 machines offered a huge leap in computer storage capacity with the "hundreds of megabytes", and finally replaced the storage capacity of the PET, VIC-20 and SID.
